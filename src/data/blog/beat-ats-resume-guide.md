# What is an Applicant Tracking System (ATS)?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software used by 98% of Fortune 500 companies to manage job applications. Think of it as a gatekeeper that scans, ranks, and filters resumes before any human sees them.

**The harsh reality:** 75% of resumes are rejected by ATS before reaching a recruiter. But don't worry—this guide will show you exactly how to optimize your resume to beat the system.

## How ATS Works: The Screening Process

Understanding how ATS evaluates your resume is the first step to beating it.

### Step 1: Resume Parsing

The ATS extracts information from your resume and organizes it into fields:

- Contact information
- Work experience
- Education
- Skills
- Certifications

**What can go wrong:** Complex formatting, graphics, or unconventional layouts confuse the parser, causing it to misread or skip important information.

### Step 2: Keyword Matching

The system compares your resume against the job description, looking for:

- Required skills and qualifications
- Specific technologies or tools
- Industry terminology
- Job-related keywords

**Scoring:** You get a match score (typically 0-100%) based on how many keywords you have.

### Step 3: Ranking and Filtering

Resumes are ranked by match score. Recruiters typically only review the top 25-50 candidates, even if hundreds applied.

**The threshold:** Most companies set a minimum score (e.g., 70%) to auto-reject low matches.

## 10 Critical ATS Optimization Strategies

### 1. Use Standard Section Headings

ATS software looks for specific section names to categorize information.

**✅ ATS-Friendly Headings:**
- Work Experience
- Professional Experience
- Employment History
- Education
- Skills
- Certifications
- Professional Summary

**❌ Creative Headings That Confuse ATS:**
- Where I've Made an Impact
- My Journey
- What I Bring to the Table
- Toolkit

**Pro tip:** Stick with boring, conventional headings. Save creativity for your cover letter.

### 2. Match Keywords from the Job Description

This is the most important ATS strategy. Here's how to do it right:

**Step-by-step keyword optimization:**

1. **Copy the job description** into a document
2. **Highlight required skills** (usually in "Requirements" or "Qualifications" section)
3. **Note the exact phrasing** they use
4. **Incorporate these terms** naturally into your resume

**Example:**

If the job says: *"Required: Experience with Python, SQL, and data visualization tools such as Tableau"*

Your resume should include: *"Developed data pipelines using Python and SQL, creating interactive dashboards in Tableau to visualize KPIs for executive team"*

**Use the exact same terms.** If they say "Python," don't just say "programming languages." If they say "customer success," don't substitute "client relations."

### 3. Avoid Complex Formatting

ATS systems struggle with fancy designs. Keep it simple:

**✅ ATS-Friendly Formatting:**
- Simple, single-column layout
- Standard bullet points (• or –)
- Plain text formatting
- No text boxes
- No tables for main content
- No headers/footers

**❌ ATS-Unfriendly Elements:**
- Multiple columns
- Graphics and images
- Charts and graphs
- Text boxes
- Fancy fonts
- Watermarks

**The safe zone:** Use a clean, single-column format with clear section breaks and consistent spacing.

### 4. Choose the Right File Format

**Best formats for ATS:**

**1st choice: .docx (Microsoft Word)**
- Most compatible with ATS
- Preserves formatting
- Easy to parse

**2nd choice: .pdf**
- Good compatibility with modern ATS
- Preserves exact layout
- Professional appearance

**⚠️ Check the job posting:** Some companies specifically request one format. Always follow their instructions.

**Never use:** .pages, .odt, .jpg, .png, or other formats. These often can't be parsed at all.

### 5. Use Standard Fonts

Stick with fonts that ATS can easily read:

**✅ Safe Font Choices:**
- Arial
- Calibri
- Helvetica
- Times New Roman
- Georgia
- Cambria

**Font size:**
- Body text: 10-12 pt
- Headings: 14-16 pt
- Name: 18-24 pt

**❌ Avoid:** Decorative fonts, script fonts, or anything overly stylized.

### 6. Spell Out Acronyms

ATS systems may not connect acronyms with their full terms.

**Best practice: Spell it out first, then use acronym**

**Examples:**
- "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)" on first use, then "SEO"
- "Customer Relationship Management (CRM) systems" then "CRM"
- "Certified Public Accountant (CPA)" then "CPA"

**This covers both scenarios:** Whether the job description uses the full term or acronym, you'll match.

### 7. Include a Skills Section

Create a dedicated skills section with relevant keywords:

**Format it for both ATS and humans:**

```
SKILLS
Technical: Python, Java, SQL, JavaScript, React, Node.js, AWS, Docker, Git
Data Analysis: Tableau, Power BI, Google Analytics, SQL, Statistical Modeling
Project Management: Agile, Scrum, JIRA, Asana, Stakeholder Management
```

**Pro tips:**
- List skills as they appear in the job posting
- Include both hard skills and relevant soft skills
- Don't pad with skills you don't have—you'll be tested in interviews
- Update this section for each application

### 8. Optimize Your Work Experience Bullets

Your bullet points should include both action and keywords:

**Formula: Action Verb + Task + Result (with keywords)**

**Weak bullet:**
*"Worked on marketing campaigns"*

**Strong, ATS-optimized bullet:**
*"Led digital marketing campaigns using Google Ads and Facebook Ads Manager, increasing lead generation by 150% and reducing cost-per-acquisition from $45 to $28"*

**Notice the keywords:** digital marketing, Google Ads, Facebook Ads Manager, lead generation, cost-per-acquisition—all likely terms from the job description.

### 9. Add Certifications and Licenses

Include a dedicated section for professional certifications:

**Format:**
```
CERTIFICATIONS
- Project Management Professional (PMP), PMI, 2024
- AWS Certified Solutions Architect – Associate, Amazon, 2023
- Google Analytics Individual Qualification (GAIQ), Google, 2023
```

**Why it matters:** Certifications are often hard requirements in job postings. ATS specifically looks for these terms.

## Common ATS Mistakes That Kill Your Application

### Mistake #1: Using Graphics and Images

**Why it fails:** ATS can't read images. Your beautifully designed infographic about skills? Invisible to ATS.

**The fix:** Use plain text only. Describe your achievements with words and numbers.

### Mistake #2: Hiding Keywords in White Text

Some people try to "hack" ATS by including job keywords in white text (invisible to humans, visible to ATS).

**Don't do this.** Recruiters can see this when they copy-paste your resume, and it's an instant rejection. Plus, modern ATS can detect this tactic.

### Mistake #3: Generic "One-Size-Fits-All" Resume

Sending the same resume to every job is a guaranteed way to fail ATS.

**The reality:** A resume optimized for a "Marketing Manager" role won't pass ATS for a "Digital Marketing Specialist" position, even if you're qualified for both.

**The fix:** Customize your resume for each application. Tools like ResumeTune make this fast and easy.

### Mistake #4: Using Tables for Layout

Tables often break during ATS parsing, causing your information to appear jumbled or missing.

**The fix:** Use a simple, single-column format with spacing and line breaks instead of tables.

### Mistake #5: Putting Important Info in Headers/Footers

ATS often ignores header and footer content.

**Don't put in headers/footers:**
- Your name
- Contact information
- Page numbers only

**Put in the main body:**
- All contact details
- Work experience
- Education
- Skills

## How to Test If Your Resume is ATS-Friendly

### The Copy-Paste Test

1. Copy your entire resume
2. Paste into a plain text editor (Notepad, TextEdit)
3. Check if information is still readable and properly organized

**If it's a jumbled mess in plain text, ATS will struggle too.**

### The Keyword Match Test

1. Highlight all requirements and qualifications in the job description
2. Search your resume for each term (Ctrl+F / Cmd+F)
3. Calculate your match: (Keywords you have / Total keywords) × 100

**Aim for 75%+ match for required qualifications.**

## FAQs About ATS

**Do all companies use ATS?**
98% of Fortune 500 companies use ATS. About 66% of large companies and 35% of small businesses use them. When in doubt, optimize for ATS—it won't hurt your chances with human reviewers.

**Can I use color in my resume?**
Yes, but sparingly. Stick to one accent color for headings or section dividers. Black text on white background is safest. Avoid backgrounds, colored text boxes, or heavy use of color.

**Should I submit both PDF and Word?**
Unless the posting specifies, choose one. PDF is more professional and preserves formatting, but .docx is more universally parsable by ATS. If you can only submit one file, use .docx for maximum compatibility.

**How often should I update my resume for ATS?**
Customize your resume for every application. At minimum, update your professional summary and skills section to match each job's keywords. Takes 5-10 minutes with tools like ResumeTune.

**What's a good ATS match score?**
Aim for 75% or higher match with job requirements. Below 70%, you'll likely be auto-filtered. Above 85%, you're very competitive. Use keyword optimization to boost your score.
